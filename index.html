"The Sahara Conservation Fund (SCF) was established in 2004 to address the silent extinction crisis facing the wildlife of the Sahara and the Sahel, an area covering over a fifth of the surface of Africa.

The Sahara has long been home to wildlife and to peoples whose livelihoods and survival are intimately linked to the sustainable use of natural resources â€“ pasture, woodland and water. Over-hunting, over-grazing, drought and an absence of resources for conservation have led to the loss or degradation of many habitats and the decline or extinction of once familiar species.

Working with partners worldwilde, SCF develops long-term conservation solutions that integrate wildlife, habitat and human beings. SCF addresses the conservation needs of some of the hottest, the wildest and the most overlooked biodiversity hotspots on earth, the Sahara and the Sahel."
